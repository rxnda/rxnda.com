I’m torn on NDAs.

Their ubiquity, and consequent familiarity, lead many to grossly underestimate how technical NDAs are and have to be, how difficult it is to write or pick a good one that fits a client need, and how much a good fit is worth. How much avoidable grief an NDA can spare. Especially when reused over and over again.

At the same time, I am, frankly, sick and fucking tired of charging clients niggling fractions of a pricey hour to confirm that, yes, the NDA they’ve been sent is what I expected, obscured by no more than the usual measure of pointless variation, and generally not worth the trouble of trying to change. Every fraction I spend that way is time I wouldn’t trade for the money, and a step backward from the kind of relationship I want with my clients.

— K.E. Mitchell
